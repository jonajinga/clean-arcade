Sudoku is a logic-based number puzzle. A 9x9 grid is partially filled with digits. Your job is to complete it so that every row, every column, and every 3x3 box contains the numbers 1 through 9 exactly once. No math is required — it is purely a game of deduction.

## How it works

The grid starts with some cells pre-filled. Click or tap an empty cell and enter a digit from 1 to 9. If a number violates the row, column, or box constraint, it is flagged as an error. The puzzle is solved when every cell is correctly filled.

## Controls

- **Desktop:** Click a cell, then type a number. Use Delete or Backspace to clear a cell.
- **Mobile:** Tap a cell, then tap a number from the on-screen keypad.

## Strategy/Tips

- Start with rows, columns, or boxes that are nearly complete. Fewer empty cells mean fewer possibilities to consider.
- Use pencil marks (noting candidate numbers) to track possibilities in difficult cells.
- Look for naked singles — cells where only one number is possible given the current constraints.
