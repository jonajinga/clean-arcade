Simon Says is a memory game with four colored panels. The game plays a sequence of colors by lighting them up one at a time. You repeat the sequence in order. Each round adds one more color to the end. The game continues until you make a mistake.

## How it works

Four colored panels are displayed on screen. The game lights up one panel to start. You click or tap that panel. Next round, the game replays the first color and adds a second. You repeat both in order. This pattern continues — each round replays the full sequence plus one new color. A wrong input ends the game and displays your score (number of rounds completed).

## Controls

- **Desktop:** Click the colored panels in the correct order.
- **Mobile:** Tap the colored panels in the correct order.

## Strategy/Tips

- Say the colors aloud or in your head as the sequence plays. Verbal repetition strengthens short-term recall.
- Group colors into chunks of 3 or 4 as sequences get longer. Chunking makes longer patterns easier to remember.
- Watch the full sequence before planning your input. Rushing mid-sequence leads to mistakes.
